Interesting facts about Great Danes
1. Great Danes used to be called the German Boarhound in the 18th century and were later marketed as the German Dogge or German Mastiff. Following increased tensions between Germany and other countries, the true place of the breed's origin was removed from the dog's name, and the Great Dane was created. 2. Despite its intimidating size, the Great Dane is extraordinarily gentle and gets along well with children. They are even known to befriend cats. 3. In the United States they are classified as a large dog breed, but in the U.K. they are classified in the Giant class of dogs. 4. The world record holder in the tallest dog category is a 42 inch tall Great Dane (Giant George). Although, the Great Danes are not the tallest breed, the Irish wolfhound tends to be taller but has a lighter weight than Great Danes. 5. The Great Danes grow incredibly fast. Within a span of just one year, they go from just a handful of fluffy puppy to being able to stand up on their hind legs and look six foot tall dog.
